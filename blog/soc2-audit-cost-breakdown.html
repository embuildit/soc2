Hidden Auditor Costs
  • Travel expenses: $2,000-$5,000 if on-site visits required

What Affects Auditor Pricing?

  • Company size: Revenue, employees, and customer count
  • System complexity: Number of applications, databases, and integrations
  • Trust Service Criteria: Security only vs. Security + Availability + others
  • Geographic scope: Single office vs. multiple locations
  • Industry risk: Healthcare and financial services pay premiums

Internal Costs: The Often-Overlooked Expense

Your team's time is a real cost that many companies underestimate. Here's what to budget for:

Project Lead Time

  • Hours required: 150-250 hours over 6-9 months
  • Role: Usually CTO, VP Engineering, or dedicated compliance hire
  • Cost impact: $15,000-$35,000 in opportunity cost

Engineering Team Time

  • Hours required: 80-150 hours for technical implementation
  • Activities: Setting up logging, MFA, access controls, etc.
  • Cost impact: $8,000-$18,000 in development time

Other Team Members

  • HR: 20-40 hours for policy updates and training
  • Legal: 10-20 hours for contract and policy review
  • Operations: 30-50 hours for process documentation
  • Cost impact: $5,000-$12,000 across all teams

7 Proven Ways to Reduce SOC 2 Costs

1. Start Early (Save 15-25%)

Companies that start 9+ months before they need their report avoid rush fees and have time to negotiate better rates. Last-minute projects always cost more.

2. Use Automation Platforms (Save 100+ hours)

Yes, platforms cost $8,000-$20,000, but they typically save more than that in reduced auditor fees and internal time.

3. Choose Scope Carefully (Save 20-40%)

Start with Security-only for your first audit. You can always add other Trust Service Criteria in future audits once you have the foundation.

4. Get Multiple Auditor Quotes (Save 10-20%)

Auditor prices vary significantly. Get quotes from at least 3 firms and negotiate based on competing offers.

5. Implement Basic Controls First (Save $5,000-$15,000)

Knock out the easy wins (MFA, basic logging, password policies) before engaging an auditor. This reduces their remediation work.

6. Bundle with Other Audits (Save 15-30%)

If you need other compliance certifications (ISO 27001, PCI, etc.), many auditors offer discounts for bundled services.

7. Use Templates and Frameworks (Save 50+ hours)

Don't write policies from scratch. Use proven templates from your automation platform or industry associations.

Smart Budgeting Tip

Budget 20% more than your initial quotes. SOC 2 projects almost always discover additional work or scope changes that increase costs. Better to be pleasantly surprised than scrambling for budget mid-project.

What About Ongoing Costs?

Don't forget that SOC 2 is an annual commitment. Here's what to budget for Year 2 and beyond:

  • Annual audit: 75-85% of your initial audit fee
  • Platform renewal: Same as Year 1 (often with volume discounts)
  • Internal maintenance: 50-75 hours annually
  • Updates and improvements: $5,000-$15,000 for system changes

Annual ongoing cost: $25,000-$45,000 for most companies

ROI Reality Check

While SOC 2 is expensive, the ROI is usually clear for B2B companies:

  • Average deal size increase: 15-25% for enterprise customers
  • Sales cycle reduction: 2-6 weeks faster closes
  • Pipeline expansion: Access to enterprise RFPs and partnerships
  • Risk reduction: Lower insurance premiums and breach exposure

Most companies see positive ROI within 12-18 months, often much faster if they close just one or two enterprise deals that were previously blocked by compliance requirements.
