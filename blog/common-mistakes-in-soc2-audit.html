Top 5 Common Mistakes in a SOC 2 Audit

Avoid these pitfalls to ensure a smoother and more successful compliance journey.

1. Poor Scoping

One of the most common mistakes is incorrectly defining the scope of the audit. This can lead to including systems that are not relevant or, worse, excluding systems that are critical to service delivery. A well-defined scope is the foundation of a successful SOC 2 audit.

2. Lack of Evidence

SOC 2 is all about evidence. You can have the best controls in the world, but if you can't prove they are in place and operating effectively, you won't pass the audit. Ensure you have a system for collecting and organizing evidence for each control.

3. Insufficient Vendor Management

Your vendors are an extension of your system. If you rely on third-party services, you must have a process for assessing their security and compliance. Failing to do so can result in a qualified opinion or even a failure.

4. Inadequate Employee Training

Your employees are your first line of defense. If they are not properly trained on your security policies and procedures, it can lead to control failures. Regular security awareness training is a must.

5. Treating SOC 2 as a One-Time Project

SOC 2 is not a one-and-done project. It's an ongoing commitment to security and compliance. You need to continuously monitor your controls, collect evidence, and be prepared for your annual audit.
