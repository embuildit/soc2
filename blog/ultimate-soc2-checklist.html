Bonus: Pro Tips for Success

Start evidence collection early

Don't wait until the audit starts. Begin collecting evidence as soon as your controls are implemented. This is especially important for Type II audits.

Automate everything possible

Use compliance platforms and integrations to automate evidence collection. Manual processes are error-prone and time-consuming.

Document everything

If it's not documented, it doesn't exist from an audit perspective. Create clear procedures and keep detailed records of all activities.

Plan for remediation time

Assume you'll find gaps during the audit. Build buffer time into your timeline for addressing findings and retesting controls.

Treat this as continuous improvement

SOC 2 isn't a one-time project. Build processes that will support ongoing compliance and annual audits. Think long-term.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid
    Common Pitfalls to Avoid
    • Underestimating timelines: Add 20-30% buffer to all estimates
    • Waiting for "perfect" controls: Good enough controls that work consistently beat perfect controls that aren't implemented
    • Treating this as IT-only: SOC 2 requires cross-functional collaboration
    • Poor change management: Lock down major system changes during the audit period
    • Inadequate training: Everyone needs to understand their role in maintaining controls
