SOC 2 Compliance for AI/ML Companies

A 2025 Guide to Navigating Audits and Building Trust

Introduction

As AI and machine learning (ML) become integral to business operations, ensuring the security and confidentiality of the data they process is paramount. For AI/ML companies, SOC 2 compliance is not just a regulatory hurdle but a critical component of building customer trust. This guide explores the unique challenges and considerations for AI/ML companies seeking SOC 2 compliance in 2025.

Why SOC 2 Matters for AI/ML

AI/ML models are often trained on vast datasets that may contain sensitive or confidential information. A SOC 2 report provides assurance to your clients that you have the necessary controls in place to protect their data. For AI/ML companies, this is particularly important for the 'Security' and 'Confidentiality' Trust Service Criteria (TSC).

Key SOC 2 Controls for AI/ML

  • Data Governance and Classification: Implementing strong data governance frameworks to classify training data based on sensitivity.
  • Model Security: Protecting models from unauthorized access, theft, or tampering.
  • Logical and Physical Access Controls: Restricting access to the environments where models are developed, trained, and deployed.
  • Change Management for Models: Establishing a formal process for tracking changes to models, including versions, training data, and performance metrics.
  • Vendor Management: If you use third-party data or platforms, ensuring they meet your security standards.
